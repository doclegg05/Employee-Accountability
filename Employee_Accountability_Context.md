# Employee Accountability v2.5 - Compressed Context

## Lesson Overview
- **Topic:** Employee Accountability
- **Duration:** 2-2.5 hours (can split into 2 sessions)
- **Audience:** ABE, ESL, HS Equivalency Prep, Career Aware, College Prep, SPOKES
- **Essential Questions:** 
  - Are you accountable for your actions?
  - How do your Habits of Mind influence your accountability?
  - How do I move from victim thinking to accountable thinking?

## Learning Objectives
1. Define employee accountability and explain its importance
2. Identify Habits of Mind that support accountability
3. Explain benefits of accountability and recognize common barriers
4. Analyze workplace scenarios to identify accountability behaviors
5. Reflect on personal accountability and create SMART goal action plan

---

## Core Concepts

### Key Definitions
- **RESPONSIBILITY** = Assigned tasks/duties
- **ACCOUNTABILITY** = Owning the outcome
- **Accountability:** Taking ownership of one's actions, decisions, and performance. Not shifting blame or making excuses—taking responsibility, learning from mistakes, focusing on solutions.

### Why Accountability Matters for Career Growth
- Builds trust and credibility
- Demonstrates professional maturity and reliability
- Leads to greater opportunities (promotions, leadership)
- Encourages continuous improvement through self-reflection

## Habits of Mind Framework
*Developed by Arthur Costa and Bena Kallick*

"What keeps us from being accountable isn't just what we do—it's how we think."

### Key Habits Connected to Accountability
| Habit | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Persisting | Sticking with a task even when hard |
| Managing Impulsivity | Pausing to think before acting/speaking |
| Listening with Empathy | Paying attention to others before responding |
| Thinking Flexibly | Considering multiple viewpoints; adapting |
| Striving for Accuracy | Checking work carefully; high standards |
| Taking Responsible Risks | Trying new things thoughtfully |
| Continuous Learning | Reflecting, seeking feedback, improving |

**Connection:** Habits of Mind strengthen accountability by helping people make thoughtful choices, take responsibility for outcomes, and learn from experience.

## The 5 Cs of Accountability

| C | Description |
|---|-------------|
| **CLARITY** | Setting clear, specific, understandable goals and expectations |
| **COMMITMENT** | Getting genuinely invested in responsibilities |
| **COMMUNICATION** | Sharing expectations, progress, feedback openly |
| **COLLABORATION** | Working together; sharing accountability for outcomes |
| **CONSEQUENCES** | Recognizing actions have outcomes; follow-through matters |

## 6 Principles of Personal Accountability

1. **Practice Optimism** – Cultivate realistic hope through challenges
2. **Practice Self-Awareness** – See things as they really are; avoid blind spots
3. **Own Your Actions** – Take responsibility instead of playing victim
4. **Be Solution-Oriented** – Think creatively; ask for help when needed
5. **Change the Narrative** – Create a new path forward with facts/skills/resources
6. **Supercharge Leadership Skills** – Accept responsibility for your output

## Victim vs. Accountable Mindset

### Victim Road (What NOT to do)
- Ignore the problem
- Deny involvement
- Blame someone else
- Rationalize/justify
- Resist getting involved
- Hide to avoid dealing with it

### Accountable Road (What TO do)
- Recognize problem and take ownership
- Forgive yourself and others
- Self-examine your contribution
- Learn what to do differently
- Act to implement new solutions

**Key Question:** Not "How do I avoid going there?" but "How fast can I get out of it?"

## Workplace Accountability

### Three Areas of Personal Accountability

**A. Your Actions & Choices**
- How you communicate
- How you spend time
- Behavior and manners
- Respect toward others
- Attitude and thoughts
- Response to challenges

**B. Your Responsibilities**
- Returning calls/emails timely
- Being on time
- Keeping spaces clean
- Living within means
- Doing what you agreed to do
- Executing job duties well

**C. Your Goals**
- Fitness/health targets
- Financial goals
- Family objectives
- Career ambitions
- Personal goals

## SMART Goals Framework

| Letter | Meaning | Question |
|--------|---------|----------|
| S | Specific | What exactly will I do? |
| M | Measurable | How will I track progress? |
| A | Achievable | Is this realistic for me now? |
| R | Relevant | Why is this important? |
| T | Time-bound | What is my deadline? |

## Scenario Summary (for reference)

### Strong Accountability Example (Maria - Healthcare)
- Verified medication discrepancy with pharmacy
- Documented the call in patient chart
- Helped unsteady resident to safety
- Immediately reported unlocked cart mistake to supervisor
- Helped newer nurse despite being busy

### Weak Accountability Example (Annette - Healthcare)
- Ignored CNA's alert about agitated resident
- Did not document the situation
- Blamed CNA when resident fell
- Became defensive during review
- Complained about understaffing

## Key Takeaways

1. **Accountability is a choice** – You decide to own outcomes
2. **Habits of Mind shape behavior** – How you think determines how you act
3. **5 Cs create the framework** – Clarity, Commitment, Communication, Collaboration, Consequences
4. **Move from victim to accountable** – Recognize, forgive, examine, learn, act
5. **SMART goals track progress** – Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
6. **Personal accountability = career success** – Trust, credibility, opportunities
